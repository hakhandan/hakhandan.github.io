Research Overview

My research is primarily focused on understanding the complex interactions between biological systems and environmental factors through quantitative modeling. I specialize in plant and animal disease epidemiology, spatial analysis, and ecological modeling. By leveraging advanced modeling techniques, my work aims to predict and mitigate the impact of diseases and environmental changes on ecosystems.

Key Areas of Research

1. Plant Disease Epidemiology

I investigate the spread and control of plant diseases using simulation models that incorporate climatic variables, host-pathogen interactions, and landscape features. My goal is to develop tools that help farmers and policymakers make informed decisions to protect crops from emerging threats.

2. Animal Disease Modeling

My work in animal disease modeling includes a significant focus on the transmission dynamics of zoonotic diseases. During my tenure as a Data Analyst in the M. bovis eradication program at the Ministry for Primary Industries, New Zealand, I contributed to the development and implementation of advanced statistical models to track and predict the spread of Mycoplasma bovis among cattle populations. By analyzing large datasets, I assessed the risks associated with different transmission pathways and identified potential hotspots for outbreaks. This work was critical in informing disease management strategies, optimizing surveillance efforts, and supporting decision-making processes aimed at eradicating M. bovis from New Zealand. My expertise in data modeling and analysis played a pivotal role in guiding biosecurity measures and minimizing the impact of this disease on the livestock industry.

3. Spatial and Ecological Modeling

I apply spatial modeling techniques to study the distribution and behavior of species in response to environmental changes. My research includes modeling species distributions under current and future climate scenarios, with an emphasis on conservation planning and biodiversity management.

Collaborations

I actively collaborate with researchers, institutions, and industry partners to enhance the practical application of my research. These partnerships are essential for translating scientific insights into real-world solutions that benefit both human and environmental health.
